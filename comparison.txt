We compare the current method with some/several "competitors" using the following measures:

- Type-1-error
- Power
- Coverage
- Interval length

The comparison methods are:

- Original Fishers (where the autocorrelation is simply ignored)
- xDF
- Pyper-Peterman
- The new method

For the computations we use simulation matching the 2014 article, namely two
AR-1 processes with lag-1 autocorrelations a_x and a_y, and inter-series
correlations of rho=0.3 and rho=0.8, as well as the independent case, rho=0.

We define persistence times tau_x and tau_y with a_x = exp(-Delta t / tau_x)
and  a_y = exp(-Delta t / tau_y).

Gaps are introduced with:
Initial spacings are generated as d_i ~ Gamma(16, 1), then normalized so that mean(d_i) = 1.

  In R equivalent:

  d <- rgamma(n - 1, shape = 16, scale = 1)
  d <- d / mean(d)
  t <- cumsum(c(1, d))

[corresponds to spacing std of 0.5]. Also lengths tried are n ∈ {10, 20, 50, 100, 200}.

Since PP and xDF cannot handle uneven timesteps the honest comparison is using
d_i = 1 for all i. To apply uneven steps we could interpolate uneven series
before feeding them to pp or xDF.
